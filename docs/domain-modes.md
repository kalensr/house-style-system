# Domain Modes

The core rules apply everywhere. Domain modes add a few checks for the kind of writing in front of you.

## Business Document

Use when the writing supports an update, recommendation, or decision. This mode also works for risk reviews and operational handoffs.

Check:

- Does the first section state the point?
- Is the action needed clear?
- Are facts and judgment separated?
- Are risks and tradeoffs visible?
- Is the owner or next step named when needed?

## Executive Memo

Use when a leader needs to decide, approve, redirect, or understand a tradeoff.

Check:

- Is the recommendation early?
- Is the evidence close to the claim?
- Is the tradeoff plain?
- Are dates, owners, and open questions visible?

## Blog Or Public Essay

Use when the writing is public and point-of-view driven.

Check:

- Is one strong idea visible?
- Does the piece sound like a person with a point of view?
- Are examples concrete?
- Are claims supported or framed as experience?
- Does the ending avoid generic uplift?

## Long-Form Report

Use when the work needs synthesis, evidence, and structure across sections.

Check:

- Does each section have a purpose?
- Does each section lead with a claim?
- Is evidence connected to implications?
- Are terms defined?
- Does the report end with a decision path, recommendation, or research conclusion?

## Informal Note

Use for team notes, quick feedback, or practical updates.

Check:

- Is the context clear?
- Is the tone natural?
- Are contractions and fragments helping instead of adding noise?
- Is warmth present without filler?

## Student Or Learning Use

Use for learning, revision, and tutoring contexts.

Check:

- Is the claim clear?
- Is evidence cited?
- Is analysis connected to evidence?
- Is the student's voice preserved?
- Are style signals treated as review prompts, not proof of authorship?
