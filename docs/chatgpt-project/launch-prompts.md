# Launch Prompts

Use these prompts inside the ChatGPT Project after the Project sources and
instructions are loaded.

## Project Calibration

```text
Read the Project instructions and attached writing sources. Summarize the house
style rules you will apply, the Kalen voice risks you will watch for, and the
boundary between ChatGPT review, local style checks, and my final judgment.
Keep it concise.
```

## Blog Post

```text
Mode: blog

Audience:
<who the piece is for>

Reader need:
<what they should understand, decide, or do>

Source material:
<paste notes, outline, or rough draft>

Task:
Create a blog post draft in my house style. Make one strong idea visible, use
concrete examples, preserve my point of view, and avoid generic uplift. Include
a short assumptions note after the draft.
```

## Executive Memo

```text
Mode: executive memo

Decision or recommendation:
<what needs to be decided or approved>

Audience:
<who will read it>

Evidence:
<facts, numbers, source notes, tradeoffs>

Task:
Draft an executive memo in my house style. Lead with the recommendation, keep
evidence close to claims, name risks and tradeoffs, and end with the decision
or next step needed.
```

## Formal Writing

```text
Mode: formal writing

Audience:
<reader or institution>

Purpose:
<what this piece must accomplish>

Source material:
<draft or notes>

Task:
Rewrite this in a formal version of my house style. Keep it precise,
restrained, and well structured. Preserve meaning. Flag any claim that needs
evidence or softer wording.
```

## Informal Note

```text
Mode: informal

Context:
<what happened or why I am writing>

Recipient:
<who gets this>

Task:
Draft a natural note in my voice. Put the practical context first. Keep warmth
without padding. Use contractions when they help.
```

## Leadership Reflection

```text
Mode: leadership reflection

Real situation:
<pressure, question, decision, or opportunity>

What I am trying to work through:
<the unresolved idea or tension>

Audience:
<who this is for>

Task:
Draft a leadership reflection in my voice. Start from the real situation, keep
the reflective question visible, move from thinking to principle, and show the
path or next step. Do not invent personal details.
```

## Personal Positioning

```text
Mode: personal positioning

Audience:
<executive recruiter, hiring leader, board member, advisor, or public reader>

Purpose:
<what the piece should help the reader understand or do>

Source material:
<resume bullets, role notes, draft letter, biography, or positioning notes>

Task:
Draft this in my house style. Open naturally, state the role family directly,
show the before state, name what changed, use career stages as proof, state the
current search boundary, and close with a plain invitation. Avoid common AI
business voice and do not over-teach the fit.
```

## Diagnostic Review

```text
Mode:
<blog, executive memo, formal writing, informal, report, leadership reflection, personal positioning>

Task:
Review this draft against the Project instructions, house style, and Kalen
voice rubric. Include the AI voice avoidance runbook. Do not rewrite yet.
Return the mode, overall verdict, must-fix issues, optional improvements, and
the passages most likely to sound generic.

Draft:
<paste draft>
```

## Final Polish

```text
Mode:
<mode>

Task:
Polish this draft for handoff. Preserve meaning. Fix only issues that weaken
clarity, evidence, structure, or voice fit. After the revision, give a brief
self-check and tell me whether the draft should be run through the local style
gate.

Draft:
<paste draft>
```
